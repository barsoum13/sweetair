Sweet Air

Revolutionizing air purification with sugarcane waste

About the Project

The "Sweet Air" project tackles air pollution and poor waste management in Egypt by using sugarcane bagasse—a waste material—as an air purification filter. This innovative solution aligns with Egypt's 2030 vision to reduce pollution and promote sustainability.

Methodology

  • Sugarcane Bagasse
  • Arduino UNO
  • MQ-135 Air Quality Sensors
  • Dust Sensor
  • Electrical Air Pump
  • LCD Screen

Step-by-step explanation of the filtration process using sugarcane bagasse.

  1. Preparation and cleaning of sugarcane bagasse.
  2. Chemical treatment to remove lignin and hemicellulose.
  3. Physical treatment to form the filter sheet.
  4. Assembly of the filter system with sensors and pumps.

Conclusions

The project effectively addressed air pollution and waste management in Egypt, utilizing sugarcane bagasse as a sustainable filter material. The results exceeded design goals, demonstrating significant pollutant reductions.
